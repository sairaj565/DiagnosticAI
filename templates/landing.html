🔬 Medical Overview

What is Lung Cancer?

Lung cancer is the uncontrolled growth of abnormal cells in one or both lungs. It is the leading cause of cancer-related deaths worldwide, yet early detection dramatically improves survival outcomes.

🧬
What Causes It?
Abnormal cell mutations in lung tissue — triggered by carcinogens, genetic factors, or prolonged irritant exposure.
📊
Global Impact
Over 2.2 million new cases diagnosed annually. Accounts for 18% of all cancer deaths worldwide.
🏥
Two Main Types
NSCLC — Non-Small Cell (85% of cases)
SCLC — Small Cell (15% of cases, aggressive)
⏱️
Early Detection
Stage I detection gives a 5-year survival rate of 80–92% vs only 6% at Stage IV.
⚠️ Major Risk Factors
Smoking — responsible for 85% of lung cancer cases
Secondhand smoke exposure over long periods
Radon gas exposure in homes and workplaces
Asbestos and chemical carcinogen exposure
Family history of lung cancer
Air pollution and particulate matter (PM2.5)
Age above 55 with prior smoking history
📋 Clinical Staging

Stages of Lung Cancer

Understanding cancer staging helps determine the best treatment path. Our AI model is trained to detect abnormalities across all stages.

STAGE I — EARLY
Localized
Tumor: < 3 cm
Cancer is confined to the lung and has not spread to lymph nodes or other organs. Most treatable stage with surgical resection.
✅ Survival: 80–92%
Common Signs
Mild cough Often asymptomatic Found via screening
STAGE II — REGIONAL
Spreading
Tumor: 3–7 cm
Cancer has spread to nearby lymph nodes or chest wall. Surgery combined with chemotherapy or radiation is the primary treatment.
⚠️ Survival: 53–60%
Common Signs
Persistent cough Chest pain Shortness of breath
STAGE III — ADVANCED
Extensive
Tumor: > 7 cm
Cancer has spread to nearby structures or distant lymph nodes. Combined chemo-radiation therapy with immunotherapy options.
🔶 Survival: 26–36%
Common Signs
Hoarseness Weight loss Fatigue Coughing blood
STAGE IV — METASTATIC
Metastatic
Spread to other organs
Cancer has spread to distant organs — brain, liver, bones, or opposite lung. Palliative care and targeted therapy are primary focus.
🔴 Survival: 6–10%
Common Signs
Bone pain Headaches Jaundice Severe fatigue
🫁 Lung Health

Healthy Lungs vs Affected Lungs

Understanding the difference helps radiologists identify abnormalities in CT and MRI scans more accurately.

🫁
✅ Healthy Lungs
✓
Clear, pink tissue with no nodules or masses visible on CT scan
✓
Normal lung capacity — 6 liters for adult males, 4.2 liters for females
✓
Clean airways with no obstruction or fluid accumulation
✓
Normal oxygen saturation — SpO₂ above 95% at rest
✓
Uniform tissue density on imaging — no ground glass opacities
✓
No pleural effusion or abnormal fluid around lung lining
⚠️
🔴 Lung Cancer Indicators
✗
Pulmonary nodules — round masses > 3mm visible on CT scan
✗
Ground glass opacities — hazy areas indicating cellular changes
✗
Pleural effusion — abnormal fluid accumulation around lungs
✗
Enlarged lymph nodes in mediastinum — indicating spread
✗
Reduced lung volume and airway collapse in affected areas
✗
Irregular spiculated masses with irregular borders on imaging
🥗 Nutrition & Lifestyle

Foods That Protect Lung Health

Dietary choices play a significant role in lung health and cancer risk reduction. Here's what the evidence says about nutrition and lung protection.

✅ Recommended Foods
🥦
Cruciferous Vegetables
Broccoli, cauliflower, and cabbage contain sulforaphane — a compound proven to inhibit lung cancer cell growth.
Anti-cancer
🫐
Berries
Blueberries, strawberries, and raspberries are rich in antioxidants that protect lung tissue from oxidative damage.
Antioxidant
🍵
Green Tea
Contains EGCG (epigallocatechin gallate) — a potent polyphenol that inhibits cancer cell proliferation.
Anti-inflammatory
🐟
Fatty Fish
Salmon, mackerel, and sardines provide omega-3 fatty acids that reduce lung inflammation and cancer risk.
Omega-3 rich
🥕
Carrots & Carotenoids
Beta-carotene from carrots and sweet potatoes supports lung cell health and immune function.
Lung protective
🧄
Garlic & Onions
Allicin compounds in garlic have demonstrated anti-tumor properties and support immune system response.
Immune boost
❌ Foods to Avoid
🥩
Processed Red Meat
Bacon, sausages and cured meats contain nitrosamines — compounds linked to increased lung cancer risk.
Carcinogenic
🍟
Fried & Processed Foods
Acrylamide formed in high-temperature frying is classified as a probable carcinogen by WHO.
Avoid
🧂
High Sodium Foods
Excessive salt intake increases inflammation throughout the body and can compromise immune function.
Inflammatory
